About

Brooklyn Timberworks is a team of traditional carpenters bringing the craft, fun, and community-building awesomeness of timber frame construction to New York City.

We're seeking partners who would benefit from a hand crafted timber frame shed or gazeebo - we're thinking community gardens, art and maker spaces, and outdoor restaurants.

Workshop participants will cut the frame during weekend classes. When all the pieces are ready the wider community will be invited to help assemble and raise the frame. Traditionally, raising day ends with a big a party!

Big Solid Hunks of Wood + Traditional Joinery

Timber Framing in the American Tradition is all about joining solid timbers (greater than 5x5 ) with traditional wooden joinery. No nails, no 2x4's, and, in our workshops, no power tools.

Assembled and Raised

All the components of a timber frame are cut to length and shape before assembly. Once that work is done the pieces can be moved to a building site and assembled in just a few hours. Frame raising is a ton of fun, can involve lots of participants, and is traditionally followed by a party under the frame.

After the Raising

Once the frame is assembled and raised, it can be enclosed and finished.
